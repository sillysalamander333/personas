ANALYZING THE RATTY DRINK MACHINES

Overview

The "Freestyle" branded drink kiosks aim to both increase drink options and simplify the process of drink vending in dining halls,
reducing the need for different dispensers for different beverages with an all in one machine.

Its drink selection screen is a digital, intuitive touch screen display that presents an array of beverages to be selected from
Once a drink is selected, a cup can be placed in the filling area
A single button is push to start and operates the dispensing
A lever in the back of the filling area controls the dispensing of ice along with the selected drink

Key Components
1: Drink Selection Screen
2: Dispensing Button
3: Ice Dispenser Lever
4: Filling Area

Observations

Most users have a similar experience in terms of the sequence of interactions with the interface: they select a drink from the screen, put the cup in the filling area, and then push the button to fill it. There was a consensus of positive experience with the speed and ease of use of the touch screen, but a couple of users noted that there was maybe too much choice and a reduction of drink options would make the selection experience smoother. Most thought it was intuitive to have a physical filling and ice dispensing mechanism rather than incorporating that into the screen, as it more closely resembles the standard design of simpler drink kiosks. Interestingly, a user pointed out a UI feature I had not known existed about the kiosk, a way to connect your mobile device via QR code and control the dispensing remotely. We agreed that it was on the less necessary side but could harbor benefits for public health in extreme measures, leaving us to assume it was likely implemented in response to the pandemic.
